01 — Scope and methodology

A control-by-control mapping, not a translation guide.

This memo maps the institutional model-risk regime (SR 11-7) onto the new AI management-system standard (ISO/IEC 42001:2023), with explicit treatment of the agentic-systems class. The output is a control crosswalk plus a sequenced remediation plan — not a regulatory primer.

The crosswalk methodology proceeds in three stages. First, the SR 11-7 control taxonomy is decomposed against its three regulatory pillars (validation, governance, ongoing performance monitoring). Second, each ISO/IEC 42001 clause is mapped to the nearest SR 11-7 control by intent — where intent does not transfer, a gap is logged.

Third, the agentic-system class is overlaid as a distinct concern: agentic systems extend the SR 11-7 control surface in two ways — they may take action without a human in the loop, and they may compose tools at run-time. Neither extension is contemplated in the original SR 11-7 text.

Methodological note · agentic class

Run-time tool composition breaks "validate-then-deploy."

An agentic system that composes tools at run-time cannot be fully validated pre-deployment in the SR 11-7 sense. The crosswalk treats this as a Class-3 gap (institutional control redesign required), not a Class-1 gap (control language refinement).

Section 02 enumerates the control taxonomy in full. Section 03 presents the crosswalk matrix with sourced citations. Section 04 surfaces the gaps. Section 05 sequences remediation against your nearest examination window. Section 06 collapses the work product into a checklist your second-line examiners can use directly.
